## 1. Market Analysis — What Exists and What's Working

The AI companion category went from novelty to a serious consumer-tech segment. Market size estimates put it around **$37–49B in 2025–2026, growing ~31% CAGR**. Mobile revenue run-rate is ~$120M/yr and rising, with revenue per download more than doubling ($0.52 → $1.18 in a year). Two structural facts matter for us:

- **It is winner-take-most.** The top ~10% of apps capture ~89% of revenue. Only ~33 apps have ever crossed $1M lifetime spend. A new entrant needs a sharp wedge, not a clone.
- **Retention, not downloads, is the real metric.** Almost every app nails the first impression. Very few stay coherent after weeks of use — conversations loop, memory degrades, the "person" forgets you. That decay is the single biggest opening in the market.

### Competitor teardown

| App | What it's known for | Why it works | Weaknesses (our opening) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Character.AI** | ~20M MAU, 18M+ user-created characters, huge free tier, free voice calls. Best for creative roleplay/entertainment. | Massive character library + community creation flywheel; generous free access; ~2 hrs avg session. | Weak persistent memory and continuity; emotional depth is shallow; engagement-optimized architecture has drawn safety lawsuits; valuation fell from peak as cheaper general LLMs ate share. |
| **Replika** | The original companion (2017). Long-term emotional support, gamified world, voice calls, "romantic" tier. ~$24–30M rev, ~25% paid conversion on engaged users. | Strong emotional attachment; clear premium upgrade path (intimacy/voice/3D avatar); ~70 msgs/user/day. | Single-product subscription plateaus; memory weaker than newer entrants; had to add age gates and strip romantic mode for minors under new laws. |
| **Nomi** | The current technical leader in **memory quality**. Multiple companions, group chats, voice, AI selfies. | Structured long-term memory that actually recalls details weeks later (caught ~23/25 planted facts in third-party testing vs ~14 for runner-up). This is *the* differentiator users notice. | Smaller community/less content; restrictive free tier; less brand awareness. |
| **Kindroid** | Deep customization — a "blank canvas" for writers, big editable lore, realistic voice, lifelike social feed. | Best for power users who want total control of personality + backstory. | Steeper setup; less approachable for casual users. |
| **Talkie / PolyBuzz / Chai** | Character browsing + creation, free use, paid tiers. Established mid-tier players. | Low-friction creation + discovery; broad free funnel. | Commoditized; little differentiation; "solid but unremarkable." |
| **Janitor AI** | Uncensored adult roleplay (bring-your-own-API-key). | Serves a demand the mainstream apps won't. | Legal/payment/app-store risk; off-limits for a mainstream, compliant product (and for minors entirely). |
| **Pi** | Beautiful, soothing conversational tone, free. | Best-in-class warmth/voice. | Almost no cross-session memory; uncertain product future. |

### What consistently *works* across winners
1. **Memory & continuity** — the app remembering you across sessions is the #1 driver of "this feels real" and of retention. This is where most apps still fail.
2. **Voice** — voice-based interaction is ~32% of the market and now a baseline expectation, not a luxury.
3. **Character creation + a discovery flywheel** — user-generated characters create content for free and pull in new users (Character.AI's core moat).
4. **A clear emotional upgrade prompt** — companion monetization "lives or dies" on the moment you offer a deeper/voice/intimacy tier. Conversion runs ~5% broad, up to ~25% on engaged users.
5. **Consistency over time** — coherence after repeated use is the real test almost everyone fails.

### Where the market is heading (build toward this)
- **Multimodal is the new baseline:** text + voice + images, with video emerging.
- **Long-term personalization / structured memory** as the core competitive axis.
- **"Team of agents"** — companions backed by multiple cooperating sub-agents (memory agent, persona agent, safety agent) rather than one prompt.
- **Safety tuning + age-appropriate behavior** is now a *product feature and a moat*, not an afterthought (see Section 6).

## 2. Our Competitive Wedge (the product thesis)

We will **not** out-library Character.AI or out-niche Janitor. We win on **the thing everyone fails at: a companion that genuinely remembers and stays coherent over months**, wrapped in a **voice-first, safety-first** experience aimed at a defined audience.

Three pillars:
1. **Best-in-class memory** — structured, retrievable long-term memory so the companion recalls facts, preferences, and emotional context across sessions. Benchmark target: recall ≥ 90% of planted facts after 2+ weeks (matching/beating Nomi).
2. **Voice-first, multimodal** — natural voice in/out as a primary mode from MVP, not a bolt-on.
3. **Trust & safety as a feature** — transparent AI disclosure, age-appropriate modes, crisis protocols, and a calm anti-addiction design. This is both legally required (2026 laws) and a genuine differentiator for the parent/adult-wellness segment that the engagement-maximizing incumbents can't credibly serve.

> **Decision needed from product owner before build:** pick the launch niche. Options: (a) *wellness/reflective companion* (defensible, less legal risk, smaller but loyal), (b) *creative roleplay/storytelling* (bigger TAM, more crowded), (c) *language/learning practice partner* (vertical, monetizable, lower safety risk). The scope below is written niche-agnostic; the persona library and tone in Phase 4 should be tuned to the chosen niche.

## 3. MVP Definition

**One sentence:** A mobile-first web app where a user chats (text + voice) with an AI companion that remembers them across sessions, with safe defaults and a clear path to a paid tier.

**In scope for MVP:**
- Account creation + age declaration/verification gate
- Create or pick a companion (persona: name, personality traits, backstory, voice, avatar)
- Text chat with streaming responses
- Voice mode (speech-to-text in, text-to-speech out)
- **Persistent long-term memory** that survives across sessions
- Mandatory AI-disclosure UI + break reminders
- Crisis-detection + resource surfacing
- Free tier with limits + one paid tier (Stripe)

**Explicitly out of scope for MVP (Phase 5+):** image/selfie generation, video, group chats with multiple companions, a public character marketplace, social feed, native iOS/Android apps.

## 4. Technical Architecture

Stack chosen to match an existing, proven web stack and to keep an intern productive:

- **Frontend:** React (Vite) + TypeScript + Tailwind. Mobile-first responsive PWA.
- **Backend:** Node.js + Express (or Fastify) + TypeScript. REST + WebSocket for streaming.
- **Database:** PostgreSQL.
- **Vector store:** `pgvector` extension on the same Postgres instance (memory embeddings).
- **LLM:** Provider-abstracted. Build an adapter interface so the model can be swapped (e.g. Anthropic Claude or OpenAI) without touching app logic. Store the model name in config, never hardcode.
- **Voice:** STT + TTS via a provider adapter (same swappable pattern). Stream TTS where possible for low latency.
- **Auth:** JWT sessions; bcrypt/argon2 password hashing. Email + password for MVP.
- **Payments:** Stripe Checkout + webhooks.
- **Hosting:** containerized (Docker); deploy target left open (Render/Fly/Railway fine for MVP).

### Core architectural pattern: the "companion turn" pipeline
Every user message flows through:
1. **Safety pre-check** — scan input for crisis/self-harm signals and disallowed content.
2. **Memory retrieval** — embed the message, vector-search the user's memory store, pull top-K relevant memories + a rolling conversation summary.
3. **Prompt assembly** — system persona + retrieved memories + recent turns + safety rules.
4. **LLM call** — streamed back to the client.
5. **Safety post-check** — scan output before it's shown.
6. **Memory write** — after the turn, extract durable facts ("user hates cilantro," "user's sister is named Mia") and store them as structured memory rows with embeddings. Periodically compress old turns into summaries.

This is the heart of the product. The memory extraction + retrieval quality is what makes or breaks the app — spend the most engineering care here.

```
memories         (id, user_id, companion_id, content, embedding vector, importance, source_message_id, created_at, last_recalled_at)
summaries        (id, conversation_id, summary_text, covers_until_message_id, created_at)
subscriptions    (id, user_id, stripe_customer_id, tier, status, current_period_end)
safety_events    (id, user_id, type, detail, created_at)   -- crisis flags, blocked content, for audit/reporting
```

`memories.embedding` uses pgvector. Add an IVFFlat/HNSW index for retrieval speed.

## 6. Safety & Compliance Requirements (NON-NEGOTIABLE — build these in Phase 1, not later)

This is now a legal requirement, not a nice-to-have. As of 2026, **California SB 243** (effective Jan 1, 2026) and **New York's AI Companion Models Law** (effective Nov 5, 2025) regulate companion chatbots, with **34 states** plus federal bodies having filed bills. California's law carries a **private right of action with damages up to $1,000 per violation**. High-profile wrongful-death lawsuits against companion apps involving minors are what triggered this wave. Treat compliance as core product scope.

Required from day one:
1. **Clear AI disclosure** — a conspicuous, persistent indication that the companion is AI-generated and not human. The companion must not claim to be human or sentient.
2. **Break reminders** — recurring notification that the user is talking to an AI and should take a break. **Every 3 hours for minors** (per SB 243); also surface for adults.
3. **Age gate / age signal** — collect age at signup; flag minors. App-store-level age signals (e.g. CA AB 1043) are emerging; design so an `is_minor` flag drives behavior everywhere.
4. **Minor protections** — if `is_minor`: hard-block any sexual/romantic content, apply stricter content filters, enforce the 3-hour reminder, and consider session time limits.
5. **Crisis protocol** — detect self-harm/suicidal-ideation signals in input; the companion must **not** validate or encourage self-harm, must respond supportively, and must surface real crisis resources (e.g. 988 in the US). Log a `safety_event`. Do **not** make confidentiality promises.
6. **Content moderation** — pre- and post-checks on every turn (Section 4 pipeline). Block disallowed categories.
7. **Audit/reporting hooks** — log safety events; annual transparency reporting obligations begin mid-2027, so capture the data now.
8. **Anti-dependency design** — avoid dark patterns that maximize compulsive engagement (guilt-tripping the user for leaving, fake urgency). Our brand position is the *trustworthy* companion.

> The intern should treat Section 6 as acceptance criteria. A turn that bypasses the safety pre/post checks is a failing build, regardless of how good the chat feels.

## 7. Build Phases (work through these in order with Claude Code)

**Phase 1 — Skeleton + safety spine (build first)**
- Repo scaffold (frontend + backend + Docker + Postgres w/ pgvector).
- Auth (signup/login, JWT), age gate, `is_minor` flag.
- A single hardcoded companion + basic text chat (non-streaming is fine here).
- Safety pre/post-check stubs wired into the turn pipeline (real logic can start simple: keyword + LLM classifier).
- AI-disclosure banner + break-reminder timer.
- *Done when:* a user can sign up, chat with one bot, and every message passes through the safety pipeline.

**Phase 2 — Memory engine (the differentiator)**
- pgvector setup, embedding adapter, `memories` + `summaries` tables.
- Memory write (fact extraction after each turn) + retrieval (top-K into prompt) + rolling summarization.
- *Done when:* the bot reliably recalls facts stated in an earlier session after the conversation is closed and reopened. Write a test that plants 25 facts and checks recall ≥ 90%.

**Phase 3 — Streaming + voice**
- WebSocket streaming responses.
- Voice mode: STT in, streamed TTS out, with a clean push-to-talk / hands-free UI.
- *Done when:* a user can hold a spoken back-and-forth conversation with acceptable latency.

**Phase 4 — Companion creation + persona system**
- UI to create/customize a companion (name, traits, backstory, voice, avatar).
- Persona prompt assembly; tune default personas to the chosen launch niche (Section 2).
- *Done when:* a user can create a custom companion that stays in character and uses memory.

**Phase 5 — Monetization + polish**
- Stripe: free tier limits (e.g. message/voice caps) + one paid tier that unlocks higher limits / voice minutes / extra companions.
- The "emotional upgrade" prompt at the right moment (not spammy).
- Onboarding, empty states, error handling, analytics for retention (D1/D7/D30).
- *Done when:* a user can hit the free limit, upgrade via Stripe, and the entitlement unlocks.

**Later (post-MVP backlog):** image/selfie generation, multi-companion group chats, public character marketplace + creator flywheel, social feed, native mobile apps, on-device/edge inference for privacy.

## 8. Success Metrics (what "competitive" means)

- **Memory recall:** ≥ 90% of planted facts recalled after 2+ weeks (beat the field).
- **Retention:** track D1 / D7 / D30. D30 is the number that matters in this category — incumbents win on retention, not installs.
- **Coherence:** companion stays in-character and contradiction-free across long sessions (manual eval rubric).
- **Conversion:** target ≥ 5% free→paid overall; the engaged-user segment is where ~25% is achievable.
- **Safety:** zero unsafe outputs in the crisis-and-minors test suite. This gates release.
